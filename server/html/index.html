Survey of linguistic complexity

C-tests are a global language proficiency test based the Principle of Reduced Redundancy and the Cloze test format. It is currently one of the most widely used, valid, and reliable language proficiency tests used in higher education for a wide range of proficiency levels (including L1, L2 and heritage languages). C-tests consist of 4 to 6 short texts (testlets) in which every second word is truncated to half of its length, and the missing half must be filled in by the examinee using general language and contextual knowledge. In order to provide enough contextual information, the first and final sentences of the text are left intact. However, little research has been done into the prediction of C-test testlet difficulty. The purpose of this survey is to collect subjective judgements from native speakers and second language learners of English, German, and Russian, and compare these ratings with computationally implemented linguistic complexity measures in order to see if the judgements are the same.
